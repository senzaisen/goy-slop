/01 — 実態

Japan's Dirty Secret 日本の汚い秘密

Japan has a food problem. The country that gave the world washoku (UNESCO-listed traditional cuisine) also gave it Cup Noodle, Calpis, and konbini onigiri with a two-month shelf life. The cognitive dissonance is staggering: Michelin-star kaiseki on one hand, 7-Eleven egg salad sandwiches that last 2 weeks on the other.

Japan is the third-largest consumer of ultra-processed foods in Asia. The konbini (コンビニ) — 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson — sell over 50 billion items per year. The vast majority are chemically engineered: stabilized, emulsified, texturized, flavored, and colored to survive weeks on a shelf under fluorescent lights.

/04 — 展示

Exhibits A through Z 日本の加工食品

🍙

Konbini Onigiri コンビニおにぎり

CLASS S

The icon of Japanese fast food — a rice ball stuffed with filling, wrapped in nori. Except the nori is factory-sealed to stay crisp for days (a moisture-absorbing chemical barrier), the rice is dosed with vinegar and amino acids for "freshness" and the fillings contain up to 9 preservatives each. The 2-month shelf life is achieved through modified atmosphere packaging, not tradition.

Rice (enzyme-treated), Nori (with calcium propionate), Filling: [Fish, sorbitol, hydrolyzed protein, monosodium glutamate, disodium inosinate, caramel color, sodium benzoate]
🍗

Famichiki ファミチキ

CLASS S

FamilyMart's legendary fried chicken-like item. It is not whole chicken — it is "formed chicken" made from machine-separated meat, soy protein, and a slurry of 20+ binders and flavor enhancers. It contains no bones because it contains no parts of a chicken you would recognize. 100M+ sold annually.

Mechanically Separated Chicken, Water, Soy Protein, Wheat Gluten, Salt, Sugar, Monosodium Glutamate, Sodium Phosphate, Modified Starch, Xanthan Gum, Yeast Extract, Disodium Inosinate, Sodium Erythorbate, Sodium Nitrite, Caramel Color, Soy Lecithin
🥚

Egg Sandwich たまごサンド

CLASS A

The beloved konbini egg sandwich. Soft white bread, creamy egg filling. 14-day shelf life. The "creaminess" comes from mayonnaise (shelf-stabilized with potassium sorbate), and the bread is preserved with calcium propionate and enzyme-modified to stay soft for weeks. Contains no butter or cream — only vegetable oil emulsifiers.

Bread (flour, sugar, calcium propionate, DATEM, azodicarbonamide), Egg Mayo (egg, soybean oil, vinegar, MSG, potassium sorbate, xanthan gum, beta-carotene color), Glycerin, Sodium Dehydroacetate
🥤

Calpis カルピス

CLASS A

The "original Japanese probiotic" drink. Marketed as healthy, containing lactic acid bacteria. Also contains: corn syrup, artificial flavor, phosphoric acid, potassium sorbate, and caramel color. One bottle has 40g of sugar (10 teaspoons). The "lactic acid bacteria" is cultured in an industrial vat, heat-killed, and added back as powder. There is nothing alive in your Calpis.

Water, High Fructose Corn Syrup, Skim Milk, Lactic Acid, Phosphoric Acid, Natural and Artificial Flavor, Potassium Sorbate, Caramel Color, Sucralose
🍘

Pocky ポッキー

CLASS B

A thin biscuit stick coated in flavored cream. The "chocolate" coating is not chocolate — it is a vegetable-oil-based compound coating (cocoa butter substitute made from palm kernel oil), containing 3 different emulsifiers to keep the "chocolate" stable at room temperature. The biscuit contains soy lecithin, sodium metabisulfite (bleach), and ammonium bicarbonate.

Wheat Flour, Sugar, Palm Kernel Oil, Cocoa Powder, Whole Milk Powder, Soy Lecithin, Sodium Metabisulfite, Ammonium Bicarbonate, Natural Flavor, Polyglycerol Polyricinoleate, Beta-Carotene
🧃

Pocari Sweat ポカリスエット

CLASS B

Japan's #1 sports drink. Contains no sweat and no natural fruit juice. The flavor is a "synthetic grapefruit-citrus accord" built from 8 chemical compounds. The color comes from riboflavin (vitamin B2 — clever). The electrolytes are pure industrial isolates. 30g of sugar per 500ml bottle. Marketed as "health" because it replenishes sodium — which you don't need unless you're actively sweating.

Water, High Fructose Corn Syrup, Dextrin, Citric Acid, Sodium Citrate, Potassium Phosphate, Magnesium Carbonate, Calcium Lactate, Riboflavin, Artificial Flavor

/06 — 自動販売機

Vending Machine Nation 自販機大国

Japan has 4 million vending machines — one for every 32 people. They dispense everything: hot coffee, cold coffee, tea, Coke, Pepsi, Pocari Sweat, Calpis, Fanta (with BVO, banned in EU), beer, chu-hi, cigarettes, hot dogs, ramen, eggs, batteries, umbrellas, and pornography.

The vast majority of beverages in Japanese vending machines are classified as "soft drinks" by Japanese law, which has no restriction on high fructose corn syrup content, no mandatory sugar labeling, and no limits on artificial sweeteners. A 500ml bottle of Fanta Grape contains 27g of sugar and is artificially colored with Red 40, Blue 1, and caramel color — none of which require warning labels in Japan.

/07 — 結論

The Truth 真実

Japan's food culture is not "cleaner" than the West's. It is simply better marketed. The omotenashi branding, the washoku UNESCO designation, the 88-year-old sushi master narrative — all true, but all irrelevant to what most Japanese people actually eat daily.

A 7-Eleven onigiri in Tokyo has more preservatives than a Subway sandwich in Ohio. A FamilyMart egg sandwich lasts longer than a Twinkie. Japanese katsu curry from a vending machine contains more chemical additives than a Hungry Man TV dinner.

The solution is the same everywhere: eat from the perimeter of the store, not the aisles. Read the ingredient list — if you can't pronounce it, Japan probably invented it. And never, under any circumstances, trust a "healthy" drink called Pocari Sweat.

Goyslop Japan is a critical examination of the industrial food system. It is not a condemnation of Japanese cuisine or culture, which has a rich and genuine culinary tradition.

It is, however, a condemnation of konbini egg sandwiches that last 2 weeks. That's just chemistry pretending to be food.

日本食は世界一。コンビニは地獄だ。
